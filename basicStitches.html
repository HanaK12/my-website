Basic Stitches

On this page, we will learn the basic stitches of crochet. These stitches includes, but is not limited to the slip knot, chain, single crochet, double crochet, half double crochet, and slip stitch.

The Slip Knot (sk)

To complete a slip knot:

  1. Create a loop at the end of the yarn with the short end underneath and the continuous part on top.
  2. Insert your right pointer finger and thumb into loop.
  3. At the same time, use your left hand to hold onto the short end of the yarn to ensure that the loop does not come undone.
  4. Now, use your right pointer finger and thumb to grab the continuous end of the loop.
  5. Pull the continuous end of the yarn through the loop and let it tighten.
  6. Lastly, insert your hook into the loop and pull the short end of the yarn unti the loop closes on your hook.

The Magic Ring

To complete a magic ring:

  1. Grasp the short end of the yarn in your left hand with your thumb and middle finger. Have the continous end of the yarn loop up and over your pointer finger so that it drapes down the back of your fingers.
  2. Take the continous end of the yarn with your right hand and wrap it up and across the other strand of yarn to create an X.
  3. Move your left thumb to hold the X at the center while you pick up your hook with your right hand.
  4. Insert your hook under the top half of the X (under the short end of the strand) and grab the continous end.
  5. Pull through to the other side to create a little loop.
  6. Now, take your hook out of the loop and reinsert it from the other side.
  7. Grab the continous end of the loop and pull through the loop on the hook.
  8. From here, follow the pattern and for the first round, insert your hook into the large loop instead of into a stitch.
  9. When finished with the first round, pull the short end of the yarn to tighten the circle.
  10. From here on out, insert your hook into the stitches created from the previous rounds instead of in the loop you just closed.

The Chain (ch)

To complete a chain:

  1. Take your hook in your right hand and your yarn in your left to ensure stability
  2. Use your hook to grasp the yarn.
  3. Pull the yarn through the loop on your hook. (You should have a loop on your hook already either from your slip knot or from a previous stitch)
  4. You should end up with one loop left on your hook.

The Slip Stitch (ss)

To complete a slip stitch:

  1. Insert your hook into the next stitch.
  2. Wrap the yarn aroun your hook.
  3. Pull through the first loop on your hook (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  4. Pull through the remaining loop on your hook.
  5. You should now have only one loop on your hook.

The Single Crochet (sc)

To complete a single crochet:

  1. Insert your hook into the next stitch.
  2. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  3. Pull the yarn through the first loop on your hook. (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  4. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  5. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  6. Pull through both loops on your hook.
  7. You should now only have one loop on your hook.

The Double Crochet (dc)

To complete a double crochet:

  1. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  2. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  3. Insert the hook into the next stitch.
  4. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  5. Pull through the first loop on your hook (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  6. You should now have three loops on your hook.
  7. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  8. Pull through the first two loops on your hook.
  9. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  10. Wrap your hook around your yarn again.
  11. Pull through the lat two loops on your hook.
  12. You should now only have one loop on your hook.

The Half Double Crochet (hdc)

To complete a half double crochet:

  1. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  2. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  3. Insert the hook into the next stitch.
  4. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  5. Pull through the first loop on your hook (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  6. You should now have three loops on your hook.
  7. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  8. Pull through all three loops on your hook.
  9. You should now only have one loop on your hook.

The Treble aka Triple Crochet(tr)

To complete a treble:

  1. Wrap the yarn around your hook twice.
  2. You should now have three loops on your hook.
  3. Insert the hook into the next stitch.
  4. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  5. Pull through the first loop on your hook (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  6. You should now have four loops on your hook.
  7. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  8. Pull through the first two loops on your hook.
  9. You should now have three loops on your hook.
  10. Wrap your hook around your yarn again.
  11. Pull through the next two loops on your hook.
  12. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  13. Wrap your hook around your yarn again.
  14. Pull through the last two loops on your hook.
  15. You should now only have one loop on your hook.

The Increase (inc)

To complete an Increase:

  1. Insert your hook into the next stitch.
  2. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  3. Pull the yarn through the first loop on your hook. (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  4. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  5. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  6. Pull through both loops on your hook.
  7. You should now only have one loop on your hook.
  8. To complete the second sc, insert your hook back into the same stitch.
  9. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  10. Pull the yarn through the first loop on your hook. (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  11. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  12. Wrap the yarn around your hook again.
  13. Pull through both loops on your hook.
  14. You should now only have one loop on your hook.

The Single Crochet 2 Together or Decrease (sc2tog or dec)

To complete a Decrease:

  1. Insert your hook into the next stitch.
  2. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  3. Pull the yarn through the first loop on your hook. (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  4. You should now have two loops on your hook.
  5. Insert your hook into the next stitch.
  6. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  7. Pull the yarn through the first loop on your hook. (The stitch you just inserted your hook into)
  8. You should now have three loops on your hook.
  9. Wrap the yarn around your hook.
  10. Pull through all three loops
  11. You should now have only one loop on your hook.

P.S. To hide the decrease better, insert your hook into the front loops only.
