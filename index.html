Home

Hi all! I have been crocheting for about five years. I love crocheting because it lets my creativity flow into a beautiful creation thatI will be able to look back on and appreciate. My love for crochet has inspired me to create this website so that other artists who are interested in crochet will be able to access all the information they need all in one place! This website strives to take all of the knowledge and resources needed to begin a hobby ins crochet and put it all in one easy to navigate website!


My Favorite Crochet Projects

This is my crocheted version of Snow White, lovingly handmade using the pattern by Que Que Crochet. From her iconic blue and yellow dress to her signature red bow, every stitch was a joy to make. She is my favorite project because of her vibrant colors that really make her stand out. If you'd like to try making her yourself, the link to the original pattern video can be found on the Patterns page.

I crocheted this tiny Santa and Mrs. Claus as ornaments, featuring Santa’s classic red suit and fluffy beard, and Mrs. Claus with her neat little bun and warm smile. Their simple, soft designs, cheerful faces, and traditional colors bring a cozy, festive feel to the tree, and working on the small details was a fun challenge. They’ve quickly become two of my favorite handmade holiday decorations.

I crocheted these two little peas in a pod as a special gift for my friend and her brother, with each pea featuring a sweet face and fitting snugly side by side in their soft green pod to represent how close they are—always together, just like peas in a pod. This simple, cute duo perfectly captures the special connection they share.

I crocheted this little hedgehog keychain as a gift for my sister, using a great YouTube tutorial with an easy, portable pattern. The textured yarn gave it fun, soft “spines,” and the contrast with the smooth body made it extra cute. Adding the tiny face and feet was a fun challenge that gave it a unique, charming personality.

I crocheted this sweet little strawberry for my sister’s backpack, using soft yarn in bright red with a leafy green top that could attatch to her backpack as a keychain. It’s lightweight and perfectly sized to add a cheerful pop of color without weighing down her bag. Simple and fun to make, the shaping and textured leaves give it a fresh, lively look and a touch of handmade charm she now carries everywhere.

I crocheted this cozy cup of cocoa as a Christmas gift for a dear friend, featuring a bright red cup topped with soft, fluffy marshmallows and a sweet, smiling face that gives it a cheerful personality. The simple pattern was fun to work on, and adding the little details—like the pillowy marshmallows and warm expression—made it feel extra special. It’s a small, handmade piece that captures the comfort of a winter treat and the joy of sharing something heartfelt during the holidays.

This wonky owl was my third-ever crochet project, made five years ago when I was just starting out, and it holds a special place in my heart. Its uneven shape and quirky little details show how far I’ve come, and looking back, I’m proud of every stitch—even the ones that didn’t turn out perfectly. It is a treasured keepsake that still brings a smile every time I see it.

I crocheted this detailed cell model for my biology class, designing the pattern myself to accurately represent each organelle with unique shapes and textures. It deepened my understanding of cell biology and made studying more hands-on and fun. Plus, it doubles as a soft, quirky throw pillow—both educational and cozy!
